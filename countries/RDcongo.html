About Democratic Republic of the Congo

The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) is a country located in Central Africa. It is the second-largest country in Africa by area and the eleventh-largest in the world. The DRC is rich in natural resources but has faced a history of political instability, corruption, and conflict. The country gained independence from Belgium in 1960 and has since experienced a tumultuous political history, including the Congo Crisis and two Congo Wars. Despite these challenges, the DRC has a diverse culture with over 200 ethnic groups and numerous languages spoken across the country.
