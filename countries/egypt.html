About Egypt

Egypt, officially known as the Arab Republic of Egypt, is a transcontinental country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and southwest corner of Asia by a land bridge formed by the Sinai Peninsula. Egypt is known for its ancient civilization and some of the world's most famous monuments, including the Giza pyramids and the Great Sphinx. It has a rich cultural heritage that spans more than 5,000 years of history. The modern Republic of Egypt was founded in 1953, and it has been an important political and cultural center in the Middle East and North Africa.
