About Gabon

Gabon, located on the west coast of Central Africa, is known for its significant biodiversity and extensive rainforest. It gained independence from France in 1960. The country's political history has been dominated by the Bongo family, with Omar Bongo ruling from 1967 to 2009. Gabon's economy is heavily reliant on oil, which has made it one of the wealthier countries in Africa, although this wealth is not evenly distributed.
