About Equatorial Guinea

Equatorial Guinea is a country located on the west coast of Central Africa. It consists of a mainland region and several islands. The country gained independence from Spain in 1968. Its political history has been characterized by significant political instability, including coups and authoritarian rule. Despite its small size, Equatorial Guinea is one of sub-Saharan Africa's largest oil producers.
