About Rwanda

Rwanda, officially the Republic of Rwanda, is a landlocked country situated in the Great Rift Valley of Central Africa. Known as the "land of a thousand hills" due to its mountainous terrain, Rwanda gained independence from Belgium in 1962. The country faced a tragic genocide in 1994, which had a profound impact on its history and development. Today, Rwanda is noted for its rapid economic growth and efforts in environmental conservation.
