About Congo

The Republic of the Congo, also known as Congo-Brazzaville, is located in Central Africa. It gained independence from France in 1960. The country has experienced periods of political instability, civil wars, and a mix of authoritarian rule and attempts at democratic governance. The Congo is rich in natural resources, including oil and minerals, which play a significant role in its economy. Despite its natural wealth, the country faces challenges such as poverty, corruption, and infrastructure development.
