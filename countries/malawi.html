About Malawi

Malawi, a landlocked country in southeastern Africa, is known for its topography of highlands split by the Great Rift Valley and enormous Lake Malawi. The lake's southern end falls within Lake Malawi National Park – sheltering diverse wildlife from colorful fish to baboons – and its clear waters are popular for diving and boating. Malawi gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1964 and has experienced various political changes since then.
